Om Nom Nomz

case study on recipe sharing

Tools

Adobe Xd
Procreate

Duration

1 week

Type

Individual Project

01 - Project Brief


Here's to the folks who've become master chefs and bakers during quarantine! This virtual cookbook project was inspired by an article about cooking with friends, as well as constantly sharing snaps of homemade food amongst friends.


02 - Interviews


I did a few rapid interviews with friends, family, and acquaintances to find out more about how people interact with each other through remote cooking. Through this, I found that people wanted a different way to interact with their loved ones online resulting from the monotony of social media. Common pain points of cooking and eating together remotely were differences in time due to geographical location and the difficulties of staying on the same steps of a recipe.

05 - Visual Style


I wanted the visual style to be whimsical and carry the sentiment of a handwritten cookbook. Additionally, I implemented a colorful, yet muted theme relating to those naturally occurring in food- yellow, pinks, and greens.

08 - Lessons + Next Steps


For my next steps, I would conduct more usability tests, as I was only able to have two, and I found there were some parts of the design that needed verbal clarification on my end. I would also want to find out if there was a demand for more interaction in the community that I’ve designed through Om Nom Nomz, and how I could execute such needs without overwhelming the users with too much information. The current high fidelity prototype is in discussion with developers to bring to full functionality.
